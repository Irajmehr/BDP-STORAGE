TOPIC: "Alt Uom Setup" — Step 2: Item CASE Sizes

WHAT THIS STEP COVERS:
Enable Item CASE Sizes unlocks the Item Alt UOM Sizes sub-page on each Item Card. This is where you define Inner CASE Count, Inner CASE Unit Weight, and total CASE Weight — the conversion math between cases and base UOM.

DETAILED CONTEXT:
A BOX10 means 10 KG of flour per case. If you receive 50 cases, Business Central calculates 500 KG automatically. Without CASE sizes defined, the system cannot convert between packaging units and weight units.

KEY NARRATION POINTS:
With the master switch on, the next question is: how does Business Central know the relationship between a case and a kilogram? That is what Enable Item CASE Sizes controls. When this is ON, each Item Card gets a sub-page called Item Alt UOM Sizes where you define the math. Take a 10-KG box of all-purpose flour: the Inner CASE Count is 1, the Inner CASE Unit Weight is 10 KG, and the total CASE Weight is 10 KG. Now when a buyer enters 50 cases on a purchase order, the system instantly calculates 500 KG as the base quantity. For a master case of individually wrapped cheese blocks — say 20 blocks at 0.5 KG each — the Inner CASE Count is 20, the unit weight is 0.5 KG, and the CASE Weight is 10 KG.
