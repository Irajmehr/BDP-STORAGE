DETAILED CONTEXT:
The Logistics Order becomes your single source of truth for all shipping documents — Bill of Lading, Commercial Invoice, Packing List, Customs Declaration — all tracked with Done, Required, and Confirmed flags.

KEY NARRATION POINTS:
The Paperwork section turns each Logistics Order into a single source of truth for all shipping documentation. Every import requires a stack of documents: Bill of Lading, Commercial Invoice, Packing List, Customs Declaration, health certificates, certificates of origin, and phytosanitary certificates for food products. Each document entry tracks a description, document type, the number of originals and copies needed, and has Done, Required, and Confirmed flags that your team uses to ensure nothing is missing before clearance. Attachments and hyperlinks connect the actual files to each record. On the integration side, Logistics Orders connect to Transportation Orders for charge allocation, so freight costs, handling fees, and customs charges are properly distributed across the products in the shipment.
