TOPIC: "Logistics Setup" — Step 5: Product Tracking & Order Splitting

WHAT THIS STEP COVERS:
Products on a Logistics Order link directly to Purchase or Sales Order lines, showing ordered vs loaded quantities. When partial shipments are needed, the Split function creates a new order for the remaining portion while preserving all traceability links.

DETAILED CONTEXT:
Your Norwegian supplier can only fit 30,000 lbs on this vessel. You split the Logistics Order — 30,000 lbs ships now, 10,000 lbs waits for the next vessel. Both orders maintain links to the original PO.

KEY NARRATION POINTS:
Products on a Logistics Order are not standalone — they link directly to your Purchase Order or Sales Order lines. This connection means that every quantity loaded, every lot number assigned, and every weight recorded on the Logistics Order traces back to the original procurement or sales commitment. Now, what happens when your Norwegian supplier tells you they can only fit 30,000 pounds of salmon on this vessel instead of the full 40,000? You use the Split function. The system reduces the original Logistics Order to 30,000 pounds and creates a new Logistics Order for the remaining 10,000 pounds. Both orders maintain their links to the original Purchase Order, so your landed cost calculations, lot traceability, and receiving workflows stay intact across the split.
