TOPIC: "Logistics Setup" — Step 4: Box & Container Management

WHAT THIS STEP COVERS:
Container cards track physical packaging linked to each Logistics Order: dimensions, weight, contents, seal numbers, and temperature logs. Box Load and Undo Box Load operations manage what goes in and out of each container.

DETAILED CONTEXT:
A 40ft reefer container holds 40,000 lbs of frozen product. The container card tracks temperature logs, seal numbers, and exactly which PO items are inside — essential for cold chain compliance.

KEY NARRATION POINTS:
Container management is where Logistics Orders really shine for food importers. Each container gets its own card linked to the Logistics Order, tracking the container ID, seal numbers, HC designation for high-cube containers, dimensions, total weight, and a complete inventory of what is inside. For that 40-foot reefer holding your frozen salmon, the container card captures temperature logs that prove cold chain compliance — your food safety team and your customers can verify the product was held at the correct temperature throughout transit. The Box Load function lets you assign specific PO items into a container, and Undo Box Load reverses the operation if plans change. As product is loaded, the system updates loaded quantities versus ordered quantities, so you can see at a glance whether the container is fully packed or has remaining capacity.
