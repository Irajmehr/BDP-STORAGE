TOPIC: "Logistics Setup" — Step 3: INCOTERMS & Shipping Terms

WHAT THIS STEP COVERS:
INCOTERMS (FOB, CIF, DDP, EXW, and others) control the division of costs, risks, and responsibilities between buyer and seller. The Logistics Order captures which party pays for freight, insurance, and customs at each leg of the journey.

DETAILED CONTEXT:
FOB Origin means freight is YOUR cost — the Logistics Order captures those charges so they flow into landed cost. CIF means the vendor includes freight in their price, so the order reflects a different cost structure.

KEY NARRATION POINTS:
INCOTERMS are not just legal jargon — they directly control how costs flow through your system. When a Logistics Order is set to FOB Origin, it means freight cost is YOUR responsibility from the moment the goods leave the supplier's dock. The Logistics Order captures those freight charges so they flow into your landed cost calculation, giving you an accurate picture of what that salmon actually costs per pound delivered. Switch to CIF and the picture changes — the vendor includes freight and insurance in their price, so the Logistics Order reflects a different cost structure with fewer buyer-side charges. DDP means the vendor handles everything including customs duties, so your Logistics Order carries minimal additional charges.
