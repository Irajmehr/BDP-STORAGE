TOPIC: "Transportation Setup" — Step 3: TMS Charges — Building Blocks

WHAT THIS STEP COVERS:
TMS Charges are the individual cost components that make up landed cost. Each charge has a Code, Description, G/L Account, Charge Type, and Unit of Measure (Flat, Per KG, Per CBM, Per Container). Common charges: FREIGHT, HANDLING, INSURANCE, CUSTOMS, DUTY, STORAGE, DEMURRAGE.

DETAILED CONTEXT:
DEMURRAGE charges hit when a container sits at port too long — at $100/day, your Logistics team needs to see this charge the moment it appears. Each charge posts to its own G/L account, so your finance team can analyze transportation costs by category.

KEY NARRATION POINTS:
TMS Charges are the individual cost components that build up your landed cost. Think of them as the line items on the Transportation Order. Each charge has a Code — FREIGHT, HANDLING, INSURANCE, CUSTOMS, DUTY, STORAGE, DEMURRAGE — along with a Description, a G/L Account for posting, a Charge Type, and a Unit of Measure that determines how the charge is calculated. Flat means a fixed dollar amount regardless of quantity. Per KG ties the charge to shipment weight.
