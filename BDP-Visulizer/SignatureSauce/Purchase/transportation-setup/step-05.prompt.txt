TOPIC: "Transportation Setup" — Step 5: Charge Allocation Methods

WHAT THIS STEP COVERS:
When a Transportation Order has multiple PO lines, charges must be distributed. Six methods: By Weight, By Volume, By Value, By Boxes, By Pallets, and Equally. The chosen method determines how each line absorbs its share of freight, handling, and other charges.

DETAILED CONTEXT:
A container has 200 cases of salmon ($120/case = $24,000) and 100 cases of cod ($60/case = $6,000). Total freight: $3,000. "By Value" allocates $2,400 to salmon and $600 to cod. "Equally" splits $1,500/$1,500. Choose wisely — it directly affects your item margins.

KEY NARRATION POINTS:
Charge allocation is where precision matters. When a Transportation Order covers a Purchase Order with multiple line items, the system needs rules for distributing each charge across those lines. Six methods are available, and the right choice depends on what you are shipping. Here is a concrete example: a container holds 200 cases of Atlantic salmon at $120 per case and 100 cases of Pacific cod at $60 per case. Total product value is $30,000 — $24,000 in salmon and $6,000 in cod.
