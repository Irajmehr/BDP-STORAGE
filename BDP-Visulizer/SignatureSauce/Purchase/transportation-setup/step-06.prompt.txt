TOPIC: "Transportation Setup" — Step 6: Landed Cost Flow

WHAT THIS STEP COVERS:
"Add Charges to PO" pushes TO charges onto Purchase Order lines as Item Charge assignments. Each charge is allocated using its configured method. The Landed Cost on Documents view shows the full cost picture across POs, SOs, and Transfer Orders. Multi-currency charges are converted automatically.

DETAILED CONTEXT:
When you click "Add Charges to PO", the system takes every TO charge, allocates it to PO lines using the chosen method, and creates Item Charge assignments. Your item cost now reflects the true landed cost — vendor price plus every transportation dollar.

KEY NARRATION POINTS:
This is where TMS delivers its payoff. The "Add Charges to PO" action on the Transportation Order takes every charge, applies the allocation method, and creates Item Charge assignments on the linked Purchase Order lines. At that point, your item's unit cost is no longer just the vendor's price — it includes its allocated share of freight, customs, insurance, and every other TO charge. The Landed Cost on Documents view gives you a consolidated picture: for any PO, SO, or Transfer Order, you can see the base cost, each allocated charge, and the total landed cost side by side. If your Transportation Order carries charges in multiple currencies — ocean freight billed in US dollars, port handling billed in euros — the system converts each charge using the exchange rate on the TO before allocating.
