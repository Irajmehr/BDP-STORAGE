TOPIC: "Config Controls" — Step 3: Posting Tolerances

WHAT THIS STEP COVERS:
Direct Unit Cost Tolerance % limits how much the PO line cost can differ from the Item Card cost. Over-receipt tolerance controls receiving more than ordered. Invoice rounding and Calc. Inv. Discount settings further control accuracy.

DETAILED CONTEXT:
Exact Cost Reversing Mandatory ensures returns apply exactly against the original invoice. Allow Pay-to Vendor Change After Receipt permits corrections even after receiving.

KEY NARRATION POINTS:
Posting tolerances are where you protect your margins. The Direct Unit Cost Tolerance percentage limits how much the cost on a PO line can differ from the Item Card cost. For our cheese distributor, if aged cheddar typically costs three dollars per pound and you set a ten percent tolerance, the system will block any PO line with a cost above three dollars and thirty cents or below two dollars and seventy cents. This catches data entry errors and unauthorized price changes before they hit your books. Over-receipt tolerance controls what happens when more product arrives than was ordered — critical for cheese wheels where exact weights vary.
